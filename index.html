Welcome to my homepage! I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Computational Science and Engineering at Georgia Tech. Before joining Georgia Tech, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from UIUC in 2018, where I worked with Prof. Jiawei Han.

Research

My research aims to build intelligent systems that help people better make use of their data for task support and decision making, especially text data. I am particularly interested in making intelligent systems more label-efficient and robust in open-world settings. Towards this goal, I and my awesome students are currently working on the following research thrusts:

  • Knowledge extraction from text: Most of human knowledge is encoded in text. How can we extract from unstructured text structured knowledge (e.g., entities, taxonomies, events) that can be used by machines? How do we extract knowledge for domain-specific corpora that are low-resource in nature? And how can we use the knowledge to facilitate tasks such as scientific discovery?
    • STEAM: Self-Supervised Taxonomy Expansion with Mini-Paths, KDD 2020
    • BOND: Bert-Assisted Open-Domain Named Entity Recognition with Distant Supervision, KDD 2020
    • TaxoGen: Unsupervised Topic Taxonomy Construction by Adaptive Term Embedding and Clustering, KDD 2018
    • TrioVecEvent: Embedding-Based Online Local Event Detection in Geo-Tagged Tweet Streams, KDD 2017
    • GeoBurst: Real-Time Local Event Detection in Geo-Tagged Tweet Streams, SIGIR 2016
  • Uncertainty and open-world learning: Deep learning models can be poor at estimating their uncertainties and fail miserably in the open domain. How can we quantify the uncertainties for deep learning models? How to make make them say "I don't know" when facing unseen tasks and quickly adapt to them?
    • SDE-Net: Equipping Deep Neural Networks with Uncertainty Estimates, ICML 2020
    • RDeepSense: Reliable Deep Mobile Computing Models with Uncertainty Estimations, UbiComp 2018
  • Weakly-supervised learning on text data: A major bottleneck of deep learning models is their label inefficiency. When there are no labeled data but just some weak supervision converted from domain knowledge, can we still use such weak supervision to train effective models? How do we handle the label noise and label coverage issues when learning from such weak supervision?
    • Multi-Dimensional Mining of Unstructured Data with Limited Supervision, Ph.D. Thesis
    • Discriminative Topic Mining via Category-Name Guided Text Embedding, WWW 2020
    • Joint Aspect-Sentiment Analysis with Minimal User Guidance, SIGIR 2020
    • Doc2Cube: Allocating Documents to Text Cube without Labeled Data, ICDM 2018
    • Weakly-Supervised Neural Text Classification, CIKM 2018
  • Composable and interpretable models: When we know the structure of a problem, can we leverage that to design more effective models? When some modules are shared across tasks, how can we effectively reuse such modules without suffering from too strong assumptions?
    • State-Sharing Sparse Hidden Markov Models for Personalized Sequences, KDD 2019
    • GMove: Group-Level Mobility Modeling Using Geo-Tagged Social Media, KDD 2016
